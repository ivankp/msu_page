Collected here are plots of differential cross sections as functions of kinematic invariants that should exhibit behavior that gives rise to singularities in expressions for amplitudes for the g(k1) + g(k2) → h(k3) + g(k4) + g(k5) process. Details can be found in Kirtimaan's note. The histograms were made from GoSam ntuples. The histogramming code can be found here. The top quark mass for the finite mtop ntuples is 172.3 GeV.

Variables' definitions:

  • s?? and t?? are Mandelstam invariants,
  • x1 and x2 are momentum fractions of initial state partons,
  • x_n_3 are the kinematic invariants in question.

Square roots of the invariants are plotted, so that units on the horizontal axes are GeV.
The signs are preserved, i.e. x < 0 ? -sqrt(-x) : sqrt(x) is plotted.

By default, k4 and k5 are the 4-momenta of the leading and subleading jet respectively.
Histograms with the reverse assignment can be viewed by selecting yes in the swap_45 column.

You might want to change to logarithmic y axis for some of the plots.

Observed features:

  • √x30 negative, sharply cuts off at −350 GeV.
  • √x31 mostly positive, sharply cuts off at −300 GeV, has a zero at 0 GeV.
  • √x32 same as above, but reverses jet assignment.
  • √x33 mostly positive, sharply cuts off at −350 GeV, has a zero at 0 GeV.
  • √x34 mostly positive, more smoothly cuts off at −300 GeV, has a zero at 0 GeV.
  • √x35 positive, sharply cuts off at 450 GeV.
  • √x36 similar to above, reverses jet assignment.
  • √x37 negative, sharply cuts off at −450 GeV.
  • No substantial difference between eft and mtop behavior.
  • Same asymptotic behavior as for non-resonant diphoton production, except for derivatives (example) and in some cases tails (example).
